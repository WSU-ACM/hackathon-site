CrimsonCode Sponsorship Information


  • What: 36-hour programming/tech competition
  • When: March 26th
  • Where: Smith Center, located on WSU campus
  • Who: An estimated 250 students from WSU and surrounding areas

Why would sponsoring CrimsonCode be beneficial?

In years past, it has been established in the WSU community that our annual hackathons inspire an unmatched passion for innovation among the student engineers here at WSU. If you decide to become a sponsor for CrimsonCode:

  • You will be associated with one of the largest technologically inventive events in Eastern Washington.
  • You will have the ability to inspire, and be inspired by, the next generation of innovators during this incredible collective expression of scientific creativity.
  • You will have the opportunity to meet one-on-one with the students here at WSU as a mentor, judicator, advocate of education, and possibly, future employer.
  • These are the engineering professionals of the future and we want them to meet you!

What does a CrimsonCode sponsorship mean?

As a sponsor, you can choose to make donations in a few different ways:

  1. In-kind donations
  2. A professor/graduate student or two to serve as judges and/or mentors on the day of the event
  3. Monetary donations
  4. Swag! E.g. stickers, flash drives, etc.

What are the benefits of sponsoring?

Naturally, the more we receive, the more we will give back. The table below shows how we will advertise you based on your level of sponsorship. The difference between these top three levels is the variance in the amount of space we will allot for your name/logo. The title sponsor will naturally have the most space allotted and will be recognized as a partner, e.g. ACM WSU Chapter and YOU presents: CrimsonCode.

What are the deadlines, dates, and requirements?

The deadline for a yes/no confirmation and declaration of sponsorship level is March 7th. If this deadline is missed, sponsorship is still encouraged, but we will not be able to guarantee your name across all mediums of advertising (particularly on posters).

Hackathons are a great way to watch students code and develop a complete project in a limited amount of time. You can learn so much more about prospective graduate students than you would in a classroom setting. In addition, students can interact with you and get a feel for your culture, as well as learn about what you look for in potential employees. What better way is there to gain exposure to students and get them interested in what you do?

If you'd like to learn more about CrimsonCode, contact Camilla Smith, the CrimsonCode sponsorship coordinator, at sponsor@hackathon.eecs.wsu.edu.

We hope we can count on your support!
