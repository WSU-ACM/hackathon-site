The EECS 3rd Annual Hackathon is coming up on February 7th & 8th! The Hackathon is a great opportunity to start a cool project, win cool prizes, meet cool people, and build your resume!

The Hackathon may be next semester, but it is never too soon to start coming up with ideas and forming teams!

All skill levels are welcome, and you can work in teams of up to 3 people to create whatever your hearts desire.

Info

Date: February 7th and 8th of 2015! More details will be posted here as they arrive.

Rules

  • Teams of 1-3 people.
  • All hardware/software platforms allowed.
  • Must be a new project from scratch.

FAQ

What is a Hackathon?

A Hackathon is a day long team programming competition

Do you really hack computers?

No, we use the term 'hacking' to refer to the programmer culture of hackers. Link for reference

All hardware and software?
Yes! Anything is possible. This is your chance to spend 24 hours tinkering and learning about something you wouldn't normally have the time for. If you think you can get even a half-finished, albeit partially broken, version of your idea done in 24hours, go for it! Just know your audience is tech driven so you may not get much notoriety for a radical new recipe for "no bake" cookies.
What do you mean, "from scratch"?
Strictly speaking, that means no previous construction, virtual (writing code) or physical (sticking arduino boards together). However, no one ever builds anything from scratch today, that is just stupid. For that reason, use of software or hardware that were not specifically created for your idea are of course okay. This isn't strictly enforced, but please abide by the hackathon spirit!
Do I have to be a WSU student?
No, anyone can participate.
How much experience is required to participate?
None really. You've got 24 hours, so feel free to learn on the spot. You may not be very successful, but most of the fun of a hackathon is the spirit and community of seeing not only what you can do, but also what others have done.
Do I have to have a team?
No, you do not have to have a team, but it is recommended. Not often do we have the opportunity to plow through a project with a good friend.
Why should I do this, are there prizes?

First of all, yes, there are prizes, but the even bigger reason you should do this is because the skills you learn from participating are extremely valuable. Also, its a great resume piece, companies love to see that you do this type of thing.

Wait, engineers socialize?

Suprisingly, yes! and if you are someone who wants to socialize with other engineers I recommend joining one of the clubs!

Is it true that people who participate in the Hackathon are the coolest people in the world?

Yes.

Will there be food?

Yes, this event will be fully catered.

Do I have to stay the whole time?

We aren't going to chain you down and force you to stay, but we are VERY MUCH encouraging that everyone stay the whole time. It really adds to the atmosphere to have everyone there during the whole event.

Well I'm sold, how do I sign up?

Sign ups will be handled on eventbrite.com. Registration will open soon!

Where will it be?

The event will be held in the Smith Center for Undergraduate Education (CUE or SCUE).
