FAQ

What is CrimsonCode?

CrimsonCode is a 24-hour collegiate hackathon traditionally held at Washington State University in Pullman, WA. Due to the on-going COVID-19 Pandemic, this year we are operating as a hybrid event. Participants will meet in-person for the Opening and Closing Ceremonies and Judging. To help mitigate possible spread of COVID-19 we ask all participants to be compliant with WSU's vaccine policy and work with their team where ever they wish after the opening ceremony. We will operate a Discord server to commuinicate with all participants. You will need to be present at Washington State University Pullman to participate.

Who can attend?

We encourage students of all majors and all levels of coding experience to attend. We will have mentors available to help you learn new technologies and give guidance on projects.

How much does this cost?

CrimsonCode is completely free as we believe that it is more important for students to focus on their projects. We will provide as much online resources and help as possible to ensure your online experience is a good one.

How should I prepare?

We highly suggest those not familiar with collaboration through GitHub to practice before hand. GitHub provides handy documentation and there are plenty of tutorials online you can walk through with your team before the event. Since judging is also in person, but hacking will be spread out, be wary of any materials you will need to transport to be able to present your project effectively. We get that it is a little bit annoying, but this is the safest option for us to even hold an in-person event this year.

How big are the teams?

A team can be made up of a maximum of 4 people. We encourage working with majors you wouldn't typically work with, or even new people you havent met!

What if I don't have a team?

Be on the lookout for any mixer events put on by the ACM at Pullman before the hackathon! Also, Stick around after the opening ceremony and see if there are others that are looking for their plus one to their team or any new teams forming as a whole. If after the opening ceremony you still do not have a team and wish to be a part of one, we will commuinicate on Discord and try to find you a placement.

What is this theme thing we've been hearing about?

In our feedback from previous hackathons, it's been noted that many do not where to go for project inspiration when you can work on anything and everything. We hear you. We are keeping the theme under wraps for a little while longer, but still feel free to brainstorm out ideas with your team before hand.

Can I work on my project beforehand?

No. We encourage everyone to start a brand new project at the hackathon. However, you are allowed to develop libraries or use outside libraries to help you with your project. If you think you might be in sketchy territory, contact or find an organizer to have us check it out. You will also be asked to provide a link to a GitHub repository, in which we can verify that all work was done within the time period.
